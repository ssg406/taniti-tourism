Get Around Easily

Come see us by ship or by plane! With numerous public transit options, hotel shuttles, and a rental car center available, getting around once you've arrived couldn't be easier.

Taniti Airport

Taniti's international airport is served by several airlines and can accommodate small propeller planes. A future airport expansion will add a runway with capacity for large jet airliners for the first time.

Cruise Dock

Arriving by ship makes an excellent initial trip to Taniti. A small cruise ship arrives once a week to the island and docks overnight, giving visitors a chance to explore more of the island than at a typical port-of-call.

Rental Car Center

The rental car center located at the airport offers daily rentals to visitors. A shuttle is on offer from the rental center to the major island hotels. Whether you need a sedan or an SUV for an off-road trip up the mountain, vehicles are available here!

Public Transit

Public busses run from 5 AM to 11 PM every day in Taniti City. The rest of the island is served by private busses. The core area of Taniti city and the surrounding area of Merriton Landing is also fairly flat and very walkable.

Bicycle

Bicycles are available for rent in Taniti City and hourly or daily rates are available. Do note that the island requires helmets to be worn at all times.

Enjoy Local Cuisine

Ten restaurants await you in Taniti serving a variety of food, including local fish and rice based dishes, pan-Asian cuisine, and American-style food. When you're ready for a night in, two grocery stores and one 24-hour market are available.

Need More Information?

Many common questions are answered here. Should you need more information, don't hesitate to contact us. We look forward to welcoming you soon!

Do I need to bring power adapters?
Power is provided at 120 volts throughout the island, so visitors from North America and parts of Japan will not need to bring any adapters.
Are there any laws I should be aware of?
You will find most laws are similar to your country, however, you should know that alcohol cannot be sold between midnight and 9 AM.
Do I need to learn Tanitian?
Many younger Tanitians speak English fluently, but in rural parts of Taniti English is spoken less often. A few key phrases will get you far and the locals are always willing to help!
Is medical care available?
Taniti has a full hospital and several smaller clinics. There are many multilingual healthcare providers available.
Should I be concerned about crime?
Taniti has a remarkably low rate of violent crime. Like most tourist areas, however, pickpocketing and petty crimes do occur. It's best to always keep your personal belongings close by and don't carry large amounts of cash.
Are there bad times to visit?
In terms of weather, Taniti is beautiful year round! However, there are many national holidays on which restaurants and other attractions will be closed. You may prefer to plan your visit around these dates.
What currency is used in Taniti?
The offical currency is the US Dollar. Many businesses also accept Euros and Yen. If you need to exchange currency, several banks are available in Taniti City. Credit cards are widely accepted, particularly at tourist attractions.
